Filicium decipiens

Local Name:- Fern tree

Foliage: Leaves are spirally arranged and even-pinnate compound. Leaflets come in 6 to 8 pairs and are opposite. Leaflets are obovate with a notched apex, around 6 - 16cm long and 1 - 3cm wide. The upper surface of the leaves are shiny, while the undersides are not. The leaflet margins are undulate. A distinctive characteristic is that rachises are "winged", with flattened sides, widening towards the apex of the leaf, such that each section of the rachis appears to be obcordate in shape.Flowers: This tree is dioecious, bearing male and female flowers on the same plant. The flowers occur at the top branches, borne in erect narrow bunches. Male and female flowers are borne in separate bunches. Male flowers have 5 stamens with long filaments. Female flowers have a globose superior ovary with a short curved style.Fruits: Trees seldom flower or fruit in Singapore. The fruits are small, shine, and reddish-purple in colour. They are oval shaped and pointed at the tip.[Others]: A small to medium-sized tree that grows up to 20m in height, with a dense, shady, rounded crown and distinctive pinnately compound frond-like leaves, hence the common names Fern Tree or Fern-leaf Tree.Landscaping Use:This tree is often selected for its almost perfect natural symmetry, and the density of its crown, which provides very good shade. It is also aesthetically pleasing due to its fine, fern-like leaves, and its compact size means it is suitable for smaller spaces in parks and gardens. It is also suitable as a roadside tree. Due to the deep shade cast by the dense foliage, only very shade tolerant plants should be selected for planting under this tree.Propagation/Cultivation:Propagated by seeds and stem cuttings. Seeds need to to be sown immediately after collecting as viability decreases rapidly.EtymologyThe genus Filicium comes from the Latin filix meaning fern. It refers to the foliage of the tree resembling fern fronds. The species epithet decipiens means "deceptive", alluding to the deceptive nature of the leaves in resembling fern fronds.
