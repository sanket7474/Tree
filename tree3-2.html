Cassia javanica

Local Name:- Pink shower

A plant of the lowland tropics and subtropics where it can be found at elevations up to 400 metres. It grows best in areas where the mean annual temperatures are within the range 19 - 25°c, and the mean annual rainfall is in the range 650 - 2,400mm[303].Requires a sunny position[404]. The plant is found in a wide range of habitats, the various subspecies showing preferences for either dry or moist habitats on a wide variety of soils including fertile volcanic loams, and from marshy, sandy and limestone soils[303]. Established plants are drought tolerant[362].A fast-growing species[404].The plant is not resistant to fire[303].This is a very polymorphic species with a wide distribution from N India through the Indochinese Peninsula and the Malesian region to New Guinea. Altogether, seven subspecies are recognized[266]. The subsp agnes (de Wit) K. Larsen is most commonly grown as an ornamental because of its larger flowers[303].
